Background

Bonsana was a faithful and tremendously devoted cleric of Selûne. He sang her praises to all who would listen, and his enthusiasm for Selûne gained her many followers due to the energetic way he spoke of the wonderful things that she represented. He argued that she should be welcomed in every aspect of their life. Every city, town and village needed a temple devoted to her. Bonsana insisted that one day, she would unite all of the races of Torin and that it would become a paradise. His love for her rivaled that of his family which included his wife and two children.

 

He organized many missions with fellow clerks and temple members to go out to champion Selûne’s message of her love of life, to both humans and non-humans, spending many months away from home, risking life and limb, in dangerous territories. Bonsana would tell anyone who would listen that they too could have the wonderful life that he was living, that Selûne had granted him, and conveyed to them his wonderful wife and children, who he often reminisced. He told many stories about their birth, playing with them, and the joy that it brought to him.

 

Then came a day, when he came back, and found his village was no more. It was gone, replaced with ash. There was nothing left. The town would not grow anything for many years to come. What happened? No one knew, or understood. Perhaps it was a raid, or a wizards spell gone awry.

 

This left him devastated.

 

He called out to her, asking what had happened, and just wanted an answer, but there was only silence. In a moment of frustration, he cursed Selûne angrily for abandoning him, and that he regretted everything he had done for her. He felt the sensation of warmth leave, and knew what had happened, he lost the ability to call upon Selûne for help, for she had left him.

 

Days passed and he ate no food. With nothing left and distraught, he hauled himself to the center of the ash town, and made a plea.

 

“To any gods that may be listening. I made a mistake. I was a fool for thinking I had made a wise investment. That I had made a sound choice. I was very wrong. I thought that by choosing my god, who enticed me with beautiful ideas, that she would in return choose me. That was not the case, and it cost me everything. I cannot, I will not, make that same mistake again, so I offer this to you. If you promise to put forth the effort that I show you, that in return I will give myself, everything that I represent, and everything that I will become, to you. I do not care what you represent, or how others see you. The only thing that matters is how you treat me. Never to abandon me like that bitch. In return, I will never abandon you, and give you all the love I had, for Selûne, for my wife, for my children. I will make my life mean something, and it will be centered around you.”

 

A god listened, and accepted.
